What Is Litigation Funding?

A financing arrangement in which a third party covers litigation expenses in exchange for a contingent return if the claim succeeds.

Funding decisions are typically based on legal merits, damages, enforceability, and risk profile. Funders make investment decisions independently according to their own underwriting criteria.

Litigation funding is commonly used by law firms, corporate claimants, and insolvency professionals to manage risk, preserve capital, and allocate litigation costs more efficiently.

Important Information

CaseFin is a technology platform that enables users to organize, analyze, and present litigation-related information. CaseFin does not provide legal, financial, investment, or brokerage services and does not act as an intermediary, agent, fiduciary, or advisor to any party.

CaseFin does not participate in funding decisions, negotiate transaction terms, recommend counterparties, or structure funding arrangements. All decisions regarding litigation funding are made independently by third-party participants.

Nothing on this platform constitutes an offer, solicitation, recommendation, or endorsement of any transaction or legal strategy. Case outcomes, funding availability, and economic terms are inherently uncertain and are not guaranteed.

Users are solely responsible for obtaining their own legal, financial, and regulatory advice before entering into any litigation funding arrangement. Use of CaseFin is subject to applicable laws, professional rules, and ethical obligations.

CaseFin is designed to support information transparency and workflow efficiency, not to influence or replace independent professional judgment. CaseFin does not evaluate or endorse cases. Information is presented as provided by users.

CaseFin does not participate in or receive a share of litigation proceeds.
